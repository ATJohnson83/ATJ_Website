Old Milburnie Farm App

Old Milburnie Farm App

Demo App Source Code

This appliction was created as two person team with my classmate, David Palmquist, for our final project at the UNC coding bootcamp. The idea for this app was born through many discussions with my friend, Daniel Dayton, who is the owner of Old Milburnie Farm in Raleigh, NC. Daniel has some basic web development knowledge and was always curious about what we were learning in the UNC course. We also discussed ways that an app could be useful for his farm buisness. Wanting the experience of having a real world client and application for my projects, I decided to turn our ideas into reality for the final project by creating a custom app for Old Milburnie Farm. David and I worked on the first project together and we also had the same tutor, so we decided to team up for this app. Please demo the app and send me feedback.

The app has six main functionalities:
Users - There are 3 categories of users: Management, Employee, Customer. Management has the ability to create, edit, and delete users. When you login you are directed to the main page of your type (management to the management main page, customer to the customer main page, etc.).
Tasks - Management can assign tasks to employees. When an employee logs in, he/she can see the tasks that they are assigned, and can mark them as completed once they are done. Management then has the ability to either delete the task, or to re-open it.
Sales Inventory - This feature allows the management to input the current inventory that is available to customers. There are 5 categories of inventory: Chicken, Pork, Vegetable, Mushroom, and Other. When a customer logs in, they can create an order from the available sales inventory, and the available sales inventory quantity is updated.
Orders - When a customer creates an order, it is available for the management to view, mark as paid, delivered, or closed. Customers are also able to see their order history.
Employee Time - This is a clock in/ clock out feature that allows employees to clock in/out from their page. Management can then go and view the employee's time. This was inspired by ExakTime, a mobile app that allows employees to clock in/ out from their phones. We used this app at my job (Whitman Masonry), and it was a tremendous improvement from the old key tab clock system.
Harvests - This feature lets employees enter their harvests by entering the date, harvested item, field, bed and quantity. Management can then go and track these harvests to add to the inventory, or use this to track historic harvest yields from different fields/ beds.

Whitman Masonry App

This Full-Stack application was custom built alone for my employer and as my second major project for the UNC coding bootcamp.

Whitman Masonry App

Demo App Source Code

This application was initally built to meet the requirements for the Second Major Project during my time as a Student at the UNC coding bootcamp. The idea behind this project originated as an attempt to create more efficiency and save time during my job at Whitman Masonry. I was assigned the task of researching and implementing an Incentive based pay system, in which employees were rewarded based directly on their work output instead of bonuses or raises. In order to do this, I had to get daily production reports from each of the foremen at every job which showed the amount of materials they installed/ the number and breakdown of their personel/ and the amount of hours they worked. After several unsuccesful attempts of getting this info, including text messages and printed out paper handouts, I decided to use my new coding skills to create an app that could potentially make this process much less painless and efficient. The first screen is a login screen. The user can either sign in as a Manager or a Foreman. For demo purposes all passwords are set to "test" so you can log in a play with the app. If a foreman logs in, they are taken to a mobile responsive page that is intended to be viewed on an Iphone (all of our foremen had iphones). They then fill out the daily report and press submit. This would ideally be done at the end of everyday. The management can then log in and choose to view reports. There is an option to choose a date range for a specific job or to view all reports. Management can also add/ deactivate/ activate/ or delete users and jobs.

Stock Market Time Machine

This Front-End application was built as part of a three person team for our first major project during the UNC coding bootcamp.

Stock Market Time Machine

Demo App Source Code

This app was built as part of a group with two of my worthy constituants, David Palmquist and James Mulling, from the UNC coding bootcamp as our first major project in august 2017. The initial idea of the project was this; What would have happened if we invested the money it takes to attend a bootcamp (approx. $10,000) in the dow jones index? And we planned to make it almost a game to see who could make the best investment. That idea morphed into its present idea; What if we had a time machine and could go back in time and invest this same amount of money in a porfolio of our choosing. We also decided to add another feature where the user could inform their investment using historic news articles from the NYT about the companies in their portfolio. The result is its present state. The user (pick Austin to test) can select a portfolio of any size from one stock up to the entire dow 30, and reference news articles about that company from the NYT over any time period available. He/She then picks a buying and selling date, and can see what the results of this investment would have been. This could be used by investors to study the market of the past, or as an education tool to show how current news can affect a stock value.

This website is my Wordpress maiden voyage. A long time friend, musical colleage, and previous bandleader/mentor of mine needed a site quick and on a low-budget to promote his upcoming new record release. I decided to use wordpress for several reasons. First, I know it's extremely popular and is a good option for creating fast, budget friendly sites and provides developers the option to use our coding skills to customize the themes provided. Secondly, I have seen many job opportunities for "Wordpress Developers" and I feel it's in my best interest to have this skill in my toolbelt. Lastly, php is a language that I would like to learn, and having the ability to inspect the php code used by wordpress gives me a big picture understanding of what and how it is used. Although I used very little coding in this project (some basic HTML/CSS additions to the template), it was a great "real world" opportunity to begin using wordpress and provided an excellent resourse for making a professional "about" website for a client in a short period of time.
